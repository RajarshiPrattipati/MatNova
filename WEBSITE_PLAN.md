# Chinoy Mathew (MatNova) — Website Plan
## "The Space Between the Shadow and the Light"

---

## 1. ARTISTIC ANALYSIS — What the Work Actually Says

After examining all ~85 images across the local folder, objkt.com NFTs, and Pixpa galleries, several unmistakable obsessions emerge:

### Recurring Visual Motifs

**Glass, Reflection, Doubling**
Three of the B18 street images use glass surfaces to trap multiple realities in one frame — the man walking past as the photographer shoots his own reflection, the woman's legs echoed by the billboard shoe ad, the street layered through a shop window. These aren't compositional accidents. They're the visual language of a mind that constantly watches itself watching.

**Shadows with Agency**
In B18 12 and 13, a woman's shadow is cast large and separate on corrugated metal shutters — the shadow precedes her, arrives before she does. In "anonymous shadows in the metro," confrontation with "darker, apprehensive aspects of identity." In "fighting shadows in my mind" (the mental health NFT series), shadows are the illness itself: *"wherever I go, my mind detracts with darker thoughts."*

**Flowers as Concealment and Revelation**
Gladiolus obscures half a face (Experiments with Gladiolus). Purple daisies hide a cancer patient's identity. A blue flower touches a portrait. Flowers in this work are never decorative — they cover pain or represent what has been lost.

**Light as Salvation, Not Decoration**
"Catching Light" — two friends inspect a light beam in a dark studio. "reach for light" — *"if we reach for the light, even in the darkest of times, there is hope."* "illumination" — *"sometimes a brief reprieve from my own voices passes me by and I try to stay with it."* Light is always temporary, always precious.

**The Moon as Companion**
Three separate NFTs titled "conversations with the moon." *"I have late night conversations with the moon, she tells me about the sun, I tell her about you."* The moon is not romantic — it's the only available company during the worst nights.

**The Lone Figure in Vast Space**
The crow alone on the beach (B18 6). The man under the tree (B18 8). "nonchalantly by the roadside" — a man who *"did not seem fazed"* by the camera. These solitary figures carry enormous space around them.

**Labor and the Body as Burden**
Workers on bamboo scaffolding without harnesses. A woman carrying rebar on her head. The body put to use, to risk. Shot in a documentary register that contrasts sharply with the intimate studio work — both are about endurance.

**Chiaroscuro as Emotional State**
The B18 11 film photograph — two figures facing each other in near-total darkness, a single beam of light between them — reads like a scene from a Bergman film. Shot on Agfa Vista 100 film. The grain, the darkness, the physical closeness: this is the inner life made visible.

### The Two Exhibitions Tell the Whole Story

**"Mural of a Muse"** (Ghent, Belgium + Artcrush Gallery, Brussels):
Created from a photograph taken of a friend experiencing psychosis at a house party. Purple light. Her shadow on the wall. The artist manipulated it in post to show black birds leaving her hand — her pain departing. *"The artwork has a sense of being unfinished, sort of like our lives and the struggle with mental health."* This piece is not about her alone. The artist understood exactly what psychosis looked like because he had lived it.

**"Phantom of My Mind's Design"** (Artverse Paris + Obscuradao curation):
Direct self-portraiture of schizophrenia. Bars across the image: the sensation of being trapped. Words embedded in shadows: the aberrant information the diseased mind fixates on, *"blended into the picture as ever-present."* A shadow behind bars: *"the phobia and fears that pervade the mind."* This was exhibited in Paris as part of a show on *"unseen and lesser known experiences of the human condition."*

## 2. THE NARRATIVE STRUCTURE

The website is built around a single concept: **descent and partial return.** Not recovery — partial return. Because that is what is true.

It is structured as **nine chapters**, each occupying full-screen scroll depth, with parallax layering creating the sense of moving through layers of a mind rather than pages of a portfolio.

The title: **"The Space Between the Shadow and the Light"**
(drawn from the visual tension that runs through every series)

A sub-heading, rendered small, almost whispered:
*"Photographs by Chinoy Mathew"*

---

### Chapter 0 — SURFACE
*The world as it presents itself*

**Images:** MG Road morning stroll, auto-rickshaw in golden hour, "nonchalantly by the roadside," sunkissed girl on the street, DSC5739/DSC5796 street shots.

**Tone:** Warm, observational, the eye not yet turned inward. Colour. Movement. Life.

**Text (fades in slowly, white on image):**
> *"I walk through the city and the city does not know what I carry."*

### Chapter 1 — THE GLASS BETWEEN
*Reflection as self-surveillance*

**Images:** B18 2 (window reflection), B18 4 (photographer self-reflected in glass while subject walks by), B18 3 (legs/billboard mirror).

**Tone:** Monochrome or desaturated. The glass surface becomes literal — we see through it but we also see ourselves in it. The artist is always in his own frame.

**Text:**
> *"I learned to watch myself watching. The glass was always there. A convenient distance."*

**Design note:** Parallax depth layers — the street behind the glass moves at a different rate than the glass surface itself as the user scrolls.

---

### Chapter 2 — OTHERS AS MIRRORS
*Portraiture as a way of understanding the self*

**Images:** B18 1 (gladiolus portrait), "A Study in Blue" (Garima), "Catching Light" (film, beam of light), "Earnest Gaze" (Bonita), B18 14 (woman looking upward), "regarding the finer sensibilities."

**Tone:** Studio blue and white. Controlled light. Intimacy and distance at once. These are people the artist knows — his sister, his friends. He photographs the people closest to him.

**Text:**
> *"I photograph the people I love most. It is the closest I can get to understanding what it means to be seen."*

---

### Chapter 3 — THE BURDEN OF THE BODY
*Labor, endurance, weight*

**Images:** B18 5 (scaffolding workers), B18 9 (woman carrying rebar on head), "concrete jungle" (shadow of tree on road), "friendly visits, different days" (birds on branch).

**Tone:** Documentary colour. Warm but harsh. These images are about the body's obligation to continue.

**Text:**
> *"The brain is an organ too. When it fails you, the body still shows up."*

### Chapter 4 — SHADOW WORK
*The first descent*

**Images:** B18 12 (woman's shadow on shutter), B18 13 (woman in floral dress, golden shadow), B18 11 (film chiaroscuro, two figures in beam of light), "standing still but always in bewegung" (long exposure, abstract).

**Tone:** Golden-to-black. This is the hinge chapter — the surface world begins to tilt. The shadows become protagonists.

**Text:**
> *"There is a version of me I cannot control. It casts itself ahead of me."*

### Chapter 5 — THE MENTAL HEALTH SERIES
*Going inside*

**Images:** Full-screen, one at a time:
- "fighting shadows in my mind" — *"wherever I go, my mind detracts with darker thoughts"*
- "my demons are always with me" — *"although I fight every day, I know this is something I have to live with"*
- "framing a better perspective" — *"drowning out my aberrant thoughts, focusing on a healthier state of mind"*
- "recycle" — *"I collect my anguished thoughts in a bag and unpack their significance when they don't hurt me anymore"*
- "by the shawarma shop" — *"I perceive as I wish to be perceived, a blur amongst the madness"*
- "anonymous shadows in the metro"

**Tone:** Near-black backgrounds. The text in this chapter comes from the artist's own NFT descriptions — these are not captions, they are confessions. Each image fades from complete darkness. Scroll speed slows.

**Design note:** This is the deepest parallax point. The background layer barely moves. The user has to work to scroll through this chapter, as if wading.

**Section header (minimal, center screen, before images begin):**
> *"I have been in the dark places. Not metaphorically."*

---

### Chapter 6 — MURAL OF A MUSE
*The collaboration between two people who both knew psychosis*

**Images:** All 6 images from the Mural of a Muse exhibition — the purple-lit shadow photograph and its manipulated versions showing black birds departing from a hand.

**Tone:** Deep violet-black. This is the most explicitly psychosis-related work. The birds are hope and loss simultaneously.

**Text (artist's own words):**
> *"We were at a house party. A purple light illuminated the wall she was sitting against. I took a photo of her shadow and edited it to express her letting go of her pain through the black birds exiting her hand.*
>
> *The artwork has a sense of being unfinished. Sort of like our lives."*

**Footnote, small:**
> *Exhibited in Ghent, Belgium and Artcrush Gallery, Brussels.*

---

### Chapter 7 — PHANTOM OF MY MIND'S DESIGN
*Direct self-portraiture of schizophrenia*

**Images:** All 4 images from the Phantom exhibition.

**Tone:** Cold. Clinical. Oppressive. This is the darkest chapter. The bars in the imagery, the embedded words in shadow, the trapped figure.

**Text (artist's own words):**
> *"The bars represent the sensation of being trapped. Words embedded in the foreground shadow represent aberrant information focused on by those with the disease — blended into the picture as ever-present.*
>
> *The shadow behind the bars represents the phobia and fears that pervade the mind."*

**Footnote, small:**
> *Exhibited at Artverse Paris, France. Selected by Obscuradao for their curation on "unseen and lesser known experiences of the human condition."*

**Design note:** Here the parallax reverses slightly — as you scroll down, the image appears to close in. The frame tightens. A subtle vignette darkens the edges as the user enters this section.

### Chapter 8 — CONVERSATIONS WITH THE MOON
*Night as the only honest hour*

**Images:** "Luna" (moon over Nuntala, Ooty), "Sol" (sunset, Ooty), "Alpenglow," "the fire before the night," "late night conversations with the moon," B18 6 (crow on beach), "follow the moon, as the crow flies."

**Tone:** Monochrome silver and dark blue. The landscapes from Ooty — the artist left the city. These images breathe.

**Text:**
> *"I have late night conversations with the moon.*
> *She tells me about the sun.*
> *I tell her about you."*

---

### Chapter 9 — ILLUMINATION
*The partial return*

**Images:** "reach for light," B18 10 (child thrown into sky, hands reaching up), B18 14 (woman looking upward into golden light), "so much love yet to give" (relative with cancer, flowers), B18 15 (glowing LED floor, disorientation into something electric), portrait of Bonita with blue flower.

**Tone:** The images brighten — not to white, but to gold. This is not a happy ending. It is the experience of *"a brief reprieve from my own voices."*

**Text (artist's own words, from "illumination" NFT description):**
> *"Sometimes a brief reprieve from my own voices passes me by.*
> *And I try to stay with it."*

**Final image:** B18 1 — the woman in white with the gladiolus half-covering her face. Eyes steady, looking directly at us. The first image and the last.

**Final text, fading slowly into black:**
> *"There is still so much love yet to give."*

## 3. LAYOUT & AESTHETIC DESIGN

### Typography
- **Headings / Chapter titles:** Cormorant Garamond (or EB Garamond) — serif, high contrast, classical weight. Italic.
- **Body / Captions:** Space Mono or IBM Plex Mono — small, quiet, almost clinical.
- **Quotes:** Cormorant Garamond italic, large tracking, centered, low opacity white.
- All text in white on dark, never black-on-white.

### Colour Palette
| Use | Colour |
|-----|--------|
| Base background | `#0a0a0a` (near-black, not pure) |
| Chapter text | `#f0ede8` (warm off-white) |
| Accent glow | `#c8a97e` (amber — for golden hour chapters) |
| Moon silver | `#b8bfc7` |
| Psychosis violet | `#4a2d5e` |
| Shadow chapters | `#1a1a1a` — barely distinguishable from background |

### Scroll & Parallax Mechanics
- **Vertical scroll is the only navigation.** No menu, no thumbnails.
- Each image occupies 100vh minimum, often 150–200vh of scroll depth to slow the viewer.
- **Three parallax layers per image:**
  1. Background (moves slowest) — often a blurred/darkened version of the same image or a texture
  2. Image itself (moves at 0.6x scroll speed)
  3. Text overlay (moves at 1.1x — drifts slightly ahead of scroll, creating a floating feeling)
- On mobile: parallax reduces to subtle fade transitions, maintaining mood without sacrificing performance.

### Transitions
- **Between chapters:** Full black screen for ~0.8 seconds. Not a cut — a breath.
- **Within chapters:** Images dissolve (crossfade), never slide. Nothing slides.
- **The mental health series (Chapter 5):** Each image fades from absolute black over 1.5 seconds. The fade-in is slower than any other chapter.
- **Chapter 7 (Phantom):** The image initially loads darker than intended, then very slowly brightens to correct exposure over ~3 seconds.

### Navigation
- **No top navigation bar.**
- A minimal vertical progress line on the right edge (2px wide, white at 20% opacity), showing scroll position across all 9 chapters.
- **Chapter names** appear in the top-left corner on chapter entry — 11px, Mono, all-caps, fading after 3 seconds.
- A small `↓` in the center of the landing screen is the only prompt.
- An **About** link (small, bottom-right, always visible) opens an overlay — not a new page.

### Landing Screen
Full black. Then — one sentence, centered, white Cormorant Garamond, large:

> *"The Space Between the Shadow and the Light"*

Below it, small mono text: *"Photographs by Chinoy Mathew"*

Pause of 1.5 seconds. Then the first street image fades in beneath — the MG Road morning stroll, warm and ordinary — and the text fades out.

The contrast between that sentence and that first warm street photo is intentional and important.

### About Overlay
When opened, a dark translucent overlay. Text in two sections:

**About the work:**
> "These photographs span eight years of walking through Bangalore and beyond — looking at the city, looking at people I love, and looking inward. Some of this work was made during periods of psychosis. Some was made in recovery from a brain tumour. All of it is honest."

**About the exhibitions:**
> "*Mural of a Muse* — exhibited in Ghent, Belgium and Artcrush Gallery, Brussels."
> "*Phantom of My Mind's Design* — exhibited at Artverse Paris, France."

## 4. TECHNICAL ARCHITECTURE

### Stack
- **HTML + CSS + Vanilla JS** (no framework needed — this is a scroll-driven art experience, not an app)
- Alternatively: **Next.js** static site if easier to manage
- **GSAP ScrollTrigger** for the parallax and scroll-driven animations
- **Lenis** (smooth scroll library) for the slow, weighted scroll feel
- Images served from Pixpa CDN (already available) + local files for NFT work

### Performance
- Images lazy-loaded, preload next 2 images while current is displayed
- WebP format where possible
- Max display size: 2048px (available from Pixpa CDN)
- No autoplay video or audio (unless artist approves optional ambient sound toggle)

### Optional: Ambient Sound
A subtle toggle (bottom-left, microphone icon) that enables a generative ambient drone — something in the register of lowercase music or Brian Eno's "Thursday Afternoon." This would significantly enhance the experience but requires a sound file or Web Audio API implementation.

## 6. THE SELF-PORTRAIT — About Section

The user has provided a self-portrait for the About section. It needs to be saved to:
`/assets/self-portrait/chinoy-self-portrait.jpg`

**What the image shows:** Chinoy Mathew seated against a plain wall, wearing a t-shirt, his head wrapped in a white surgical bandage/dressing — the unmistakable marker of a brain surgery. His hands are pressed together in a steepled prayer gesture at chin height. He looks directly into the camera. The expression is not suffering, not smiling. It is steady. Unflinching. Present.

This is the most important image in the entire body of work. Not because it is technically the best — it is shot on a phone — but because it is the most honest. It is the origin point of everything. The brain that makes the photographs, temporarily altered by the tumour. The hands that press together — whether in prayer, in patience, or in the gesture a person makes when they are composing a thought they need time to land carefully.

**About Section Design:**
- Appears after the full scroll journey is complete — accessed via the About link (bottom-right)
- Dark overlay slides in from right
- Self-portrait: large, slightly desaturated, left side of screen
- Text, right side, in Cormorant Garamond italic:

> *"My name is Chinoy Mathew. I am a photographer based in Bangalore, India.*
>
> *I have been living with schizophrenia for many years. In 2024, I was diagnosed with a brain tumour. I am recovering.*
>
> *I photograph what I see — the city, the people I love, and the interior of a mind that does not always behave.*
>
> *Two of my works have been exhibited internationally: 'Mural of a Muse' in Ghent and Brussels, and 'Phantom of My Mind's Design' in Paris.*
>
> *Photography is how I stay."*

## 8. WHAT THIS WEBSITE IS NOT

- It is not a portfolio grid.
- It is not a slideshow.
- It is not structured around categories or dates.
- It does not have thumbnails, hover states, or "click to enlarge."
- It does not begin with an artist bio.
- It does not use the word "photography" in the UI.

It is a walk through a mind that has seen dark places and has, occasionally, found light — and has the photographs to prove both.
