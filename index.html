The Space Between
the Shadow and the Light

Photographs by Chinoy Mathew

↓

I walk through the city
and the city does not know
what I carry.

I learned to watch myself watching.
The glass was always there.
A convenient distance.

I photograph the people I love most.
It is the closest I can get
to understanding what it means
to be seen.

The brain is an organ too.
When it fails you,
the body still shows up.

There is a version of me
I cannot control.
It casts itself ahead of me.

I have been in the dark places.
Not metaphorically.

fighting shadows in my mind

"wherever I go, my mind detracts with darker thoughts"

my demons are always with me

"although I fight every day, I know this is something I have to live with"

framing a better perspective

"drowning out my aberrant thoughts and focusing on a healthier state of mind"

recycle

"I collect my anguished thoughts in a bag and unpack their significance when they don't hurt me anymore"

by the shawarma shop

"I perceive as I wish to be perceived, a blur amongst the madness"

anonymous shadows in the metro

prologue

"an ominous figure approaches"

We were at a house party.
A purple light illuminated the wall
she was sitting against.

I took a photo of her shadow
and edited it to express
her letting go of her pain
through the black birds
exiting her hand.

"The artwork has a sense of being unfinished.
Sort of like our lives."

I have late night conversations with the moon.
She tells me about the sun.
I tell her about you.

"Sometimes a brief reprieve
from my own voices passes me by.
And I try to stay with it."

"if we reach for the light,
even in the darkest of times,
there is hope"

There is still so much love yet to give.

Chinoy Mathew

My name is Chinoy Mathew. I am a photographer based in Bangalore, India.

I have been living with schizophrenia for many years. In 2024, I was diagnosed with a brain tumour. I am recovering.

I photograph what I see — the city, the people I love, and the interior of a mind that does not always behave.

Two of my works have been exhibited internationally: Mural of a Muse in Ghent and Brussels, and Phantom of My Mind's Design in Paris.

Photography is how I stay.
